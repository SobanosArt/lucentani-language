# lucentani-language

Luçentani is a constructed Romance bridge language
designed to unify elements of Portuguese, Spanish,
Galician, and Italian.

Luçentani can be written in both Roman and Cyrillic alphabets

New rule a, e, o strong vowels
I and u are weak

diminutive - iña/iño caro -> cariño
also - eto    neno -> neniño   ->neniñeto

Luçentani is a bridge language, naturally understood by native speakers of Italian, 
Spanish, Portuguese, and Galician. Its vocabulary has been carefully selected to also 
appear familiar to English speakers, featuring regularized and normalized verbs that 
follow consistent patterns across all tenses. In some ways, Luçentani resembles Portuguese,
then suddenly feels like simplified Spanish.

Its phonology is crafted to sound comfortable to English-speaking ears. For example, 
sounds like "nation" are spelled as naxión - pronounced “na shión” or “na shun.”

Dialects are encouraged to add flavor and personality to spoken Luçentani. Speakers can 
soften or harden their “R” sounds to their comfort, while “L” sounds range from dark 
(like European Portuguese) to nearly silent (as in Brazilian Portuguese). This flexibility 
makes speaking Luçentani fun and easy.

The verb ser is the dominant form of “to be,” while estar is used in specific contexts 
with gerund forms, which are fully regular and standardized. The verb teñer functions 
like its French and Portuguese counterparts, with haver used only in the third person 
to express generic existence (there is/are).

Instead of a subjunctive case, Luçentani generally uses verbal phrases; however, 
a subjunctive tense does exist for poetic or religious contexts.


## Features

• simplified grammar  
• regular verb system  
• articles def: le / les   le casa, le carro, les casas, les carros
• articles indef: un / uns  un homen, uns homens, un casa, uns casas
• inclusive pronoun: on  - indicates "we"


Conjugation for ser (to be), var (to go) and har (to have)

present tense adds o, as, a, amo, an
Example 
so - ses - sé - semo - sen
vo - vas - va - vamo - van
ho - has - a - hamo - ham
